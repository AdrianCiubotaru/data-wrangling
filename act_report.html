Wrangle Doggos¶

Insights and visualisations

Data wrangling is often the most time consuming process in any data analysis project. But it is fundamental in achieving the goals of any project, which is to uncover solutions into real life problems based on data. After going through this process, the foundations are layed for the actual purpose of the project. Bellow I will list some of the questions which came into my mind when I first saw the available data set:

  • Which dog stage is being favorited the most on average?
  • Which dog stage is being retweeted the most on average?
  • What is the average rating for all dog stages?

With these questions in mind, I was able to better asses and clean the data I had at my disposal, as well as to understand what other data needed to be gathered. Specifically, I had to pay close attention to the dog stages and I needed to be able to extract the correct ratings from the full_text.

Of course, having a flat number for each dog stage is not very helpful. This state of affairs can definitely be improved with some visualisations. I believe a boxplot is a good choice in this situation, because it can show some of the spread within the data for each dog stage. Also, because the rating is on a completely arbitrary scale, I will only perform the visualisations for the favorite_count and retweet_count, and I will remove the top 10% of the values.

Of course, moving forwards, additional analysis can be done on this data set. These visualisations alone might be misleading , with them showing a clear difference between puppos and the other dog stages. In order to determine if the difference between the dog stages favorite_count and retwee_count are statistically relevant, a z-test can be performed, but not before transforming the data so that it has a normal distribution.
